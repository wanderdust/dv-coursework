Director of Research

These visualizations are designed for a Director of Research of a Unit of Assessment within a university. They will be able to browse different universitites and locations to see what units of assesment revieve better ratings than other. By investigating this, they will be able to make informed decisions on how to write the REF 5 submissions to maximise their REF Assessment.

In the first graph you can choose a unit of assessment for any university to see their overall ratings, from 1 to 4 stars per subject. The map on the right shows the location of any of the selected universities. The map also shows the 4-star density by region.

In the bubble graph you will see the subjects per region with the most 4 star rating. The larger the bubble for a Unit Of Assessment, the more 4 stars it has. The user can choose to see overall, impact, outputs or environment ratings. On the bar plot on the right, the user has a more detailed view of the overall ratings a user revieved in the university selected.

This chart shows a topic to topic correlation based on how often they appear together. The Director of Research can hover on top of any square and it will tell them how correlated they are. Red squares mean that the values are more correlated than white squares.
